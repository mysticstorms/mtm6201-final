About Us

Get to know who we are

Our Mission

Our mission here at Pawster is to provide protection, care, and a true second chance at life for the animals in our community. We are a no-kill facility and believe that all animals deserve a sense of safety, care, and a chance to find a loving home.

We believe in improving the lives of animals in our community through quality medical care, a dedicated staff, an amazing team of volunteers, and community outreach. We are committed to promoting a sense of kindness, understanding, and connection to all animals and families alike, and to building a more compassionate and connected community for all.

Our Values

Respect

Respecting everyone for who they are by building a healthy, positive, safe and caring environment for our members and our community.

Liability

We ensure to stay liable with our decisions, actions and mistakes. We always ensure that our community and pets are well taken cared of.

Community Involvement

We ensure to stay actively involved in ensuring the growth and development of our community to optimize animal welfare!

Team well-being

We always maintain a balanced and healthy working climate and motivate the personal and professional development of our employees and volunteers.

Our Story

Our story begins with the simple belief that every animal deserves a safe place to go, a second chance at life, and someone who will not give up on them. We began as a group of animal lovers helping stray and abandoned pets in our community. We quickly expanded as we received requests for help from our neighbors. We also received offers of help from volunteers in our community and the city. We have worked tirelessly to build Pawster, not only as a shelter but as a promise to those animals that have nowhere else to turn. We have made the conscious choice to be a no-kill shelter because every life is precious and every animal deserves to have a loving home. We believe that safety, compassion, and patience guide everything we do. Our shelter is run entirely by volunteers. They help us stay connected to our community.
